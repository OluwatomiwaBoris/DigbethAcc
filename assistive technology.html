Assistive Software Solutions for Users with Sight Loss



Assistive technology has revolutionized the way individuals with sight loss can interact with computers and digital content.

Computer Access for the Visually Impaired

Assistive technology has revolutionized the way individuals with sight loss can interact with computers and digital content. There are various software options available that cater to different needs and preferences. This page highlights two prominent assistive software tools: one that is paid and another that is free.

Paid Softw​aree:

An overview of 
JAWS (Job Access With Speech) .

JAWS (Job Access With​ Speech).

JAWS is one of the most popular and powerful screen readers available for users with sight loss. Developed by Freedom Scientific, JAWS converts text and other screen elements into speech or Braille, enabling users to navigate their computer and the internet with ease.

Key features
  • Text-to-speech
  • Braille Support
  • Customizable
  • Seamless Integration
  • OCR Capability

Cost

JAWS is a premium software solution, with pricing typically starting around $90 for a 90-day license and $95 per year for an annual Home License.

Free Softw​are:

An overview of 
NVDA (Non Visual Desktop Access) .

JAWS (Job Access With​ Speech).

NVDA is a free, open-source screen reader developed by NV Access. It provides essential functionality for navigating and interacting with computers without sight, making it an excellent option for users who need a cost-effective solution.

Summary 

Assistive Technology

Both JAWS and NVDA offer invaluable tools for individuals with sight loss, enhancing their ability to use computers effectively. While JAWS provides a comprehensive and highly customizable experience at a premium price, NVDA offers a robust and cost-free alternative. The choice between them will depend on the user's specific needs, preferences, and budget. By leveraging these technologies, users with sight loss can significantly improve their digital literacy and independence.
